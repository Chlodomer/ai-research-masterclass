# Presenter Notes: AI for Research Master Class

**Date:** April 16, 2026 | **Duration:** 2.5 hours | **Audience:** ~25 senior faculty, cross-disciplinary

---

## Before You Start (5 min before)

- QR code on screen (projector)
- Landing page open on your laptop: chlodomer.github.io/ai-research-masterclass
- Have demo-filled.html open in a tab for showing the context brief output
- Have a ChatGPT conversation ready with the demo context brief pasted (the immunology example), so you can show the AI's response live
- Wifi credentials on the board if relevant

---

## Opening Framing (15 min)

### The Setup (5 min)

- "You already know your research. I'm not here to teach you how to think about it."
- "I'm here to teach you how to make AI useful for the work you already do."
- "The problem: when you open ChatGPT and type 'help me write a grant proposal,' you get generic garbage. Why? Because the AI knows nothing about you, your field, your terms, your boundaries."
- "Today we fix that. The core skill is context engineering: translating what's in your head into structured input the AI can actually work with."

### The Demo (5 min)

- Show the demo-filled context brief (demo-filled.html)
- Scroll through: "This took someone 10 minutes to fill out. It's not teaching them research. It's making their expertise machine-legible."
- Switch to ChatGPT tab, show the AI's response to the pasted brief
- "Notice: it already knows the vocabulary, the scholars, the boundaries, the aims. One paste. No back-and-forth."

### The Plan (5 min)

- Show the landing page, point to the four phases
- "We go from context brief to bibliography to synthesis to a proposal draft. Each phase feeds the next. Everything accumulates in a project."
- "You'll work on your own research topic throughout. I'll demo each step first, then you do it."
- **Show QR code.** "Scan this. Everything you need is there."

---

## Phase 1: Context Brief + Project Setup (25 min)

### Your Demo (3 min)

- Show the context brief form (context-brief.html)
- Walk through 2-3 fields: "This isn't a research plan. It's a translation layer. The 'Key Terms' section is where you correct the AI before it gets things wrong."
- Show the "Generate" button and the output

### They Work (12 min)

- "Open the form on your phone or laptop. Fill it out using your own research. You already know the answers; the work is making them explicit."
- Circulate. Common issues: people write too little in the terms section, or skip the "do not search for" field. Push them on both.

### Project Setup (10 min)

- Demo: create a project in ChatGPT (or show Claude/Gemini equivalent on screen)
- Demo: paste context brief, show response
- Demo: ask AI to generate system prompt, paste into project settings
- Demo: test with "What do you know about my research?"
- They do it. Circulate. Help with platform-specific issues.

---

## Phase 2: Bibliography Discovery (35 min)

### Level 1: Basic Search (15 min)

- Demo the Level 1 prompt from the workshop guide (5 min)
- Show results. Point out: "Notice how the constraints from the context brief shaped what it found? No yogurt probiotics. No gut-brain papers."
- They do it with their own topics (10 min)

### Verification: The Critical Lesson (10 min)

- **This is the most important 10 minutes of the workshop.**
- Open verification.html on the projector
- Live demo: take one citation from the AI's results, walk through all 5 gates on screen
  - Click the DOI
  - Compare the title
  - Check the authors
  - Read the abstract, compare to what the AI claimed
  - Try to access full text
- If you find a hallucinated or mischaracterized citation, that's gold. Show it.
- If all citations check out, say: "Today was a good day. It won't always be. The method exists for the days it isn't."
- **Key line:** "Trust is not a method. If you haven't clicked the DOI and read the abstract, you haven't verified it."

### Level 2: Deep Research (10 min)

- Demo the Level 2a prompt: ask AI to generate a deep research prompt from context
- Show the output. "Look at how it incorporated your terms and boundaries automatically."
- Launch a deep research task live. "This will take 15-20 minutes. We'll check on it after the break."
- Mention: they can do this at home with more time

---

## Break (10 min)

- Check on the deep research task during the break
- Circulate, answer questions

---

## Phase 3: Synthesis (30 min)

### Upload and Synthesize (15 min)

- Demo: download a PDF from the bibliography results, upload to the conversation
- Demo the Level 3 prompt: "Read these papers. What's the consensus? Where do they disagree? What gap does my project fill?"
- Show the output. Point out how it cites by author name.

### Background Section Draft (15 min)

- Demo the Phase 3 Step 1 prompt: "Draft a Background and Significance section"
- Show the output. Point out the structure.
- Demo iteration: "The connection between X and Y is overstated. Soften it." Show how specific feedback produces specific revision.
- They do it. If they haven't uploaded papers yet, they can work from the bibliography results.

---

## Phase 4: Proposal Generation (30 min)

### Research Plan (15 min)

- Demo the Phase 4 Step 1 prompt: "Draft the Research Plan section"
- Show the output. Walk through one aim: rationale, methodology, expected results, pitfalls.
- Point out: "Look at the pitfalls section. The AI knows to include this because we specified the structure. Without the structure instruction, you get a cheerful plan with no risk assessment."
- They work on their own.

### Reviewer Simulation (10 min)

- Demo the Phase 4 Step 2 prompt: "Act as a critical ISF reviewer"
- Show the output. "It found three weaknesses I didn't see."
- "This is the antidote to AI sycophancy. By default, the AI will tell you your proposal is brilliant. You have to ask it to be harsh."
- They try it.

### Wrap Discussion (5 min)

- "What surprised you today?"
- "What are you going to use first?"
- Let 3-4 people share briefly.

---

## Closing (10 min)

- Show the landing page one more time
- "Everything is at this URL. The context brief form, the prompt guide, the verification checklist, the ISF reference. It's all there."
- "The core takeaway: context engineering is the skill. Not prompting. Not tool selection. The ability to translate your expertise into structured input that makes the AI genuinely useful."
- "The context brief you built today is a working foundation. Go home, continue the conversation, iterate on the proposal. The project saves your state."
- **QR code on screen one last time.**
- "Thank you."

---

## Emergency Notes

- **If wifi dies:** Have them use mobile hotspot. The context brief form works offline once loaded.
- **If someone has no AI subscription:** Pair them with a neighbor. The exercises work in pairs.
- **If deep research takes too long:** Skip it, move to Phase 3 using the Level 1 bibliography results.
- **If the demo AI doesn't hallucinate:** "Today the AI was honest. It won't always be. That's precisely why the verification method needs to be a habit, not a reaction."
