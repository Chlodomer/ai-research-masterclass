# ISF Proposal Structure: Personal Research Grant (New PI Track)

Upload this file to your AI project so the AI knows the ISF format when helping you draft proposal sections.

---

## Key Deadlines

- **Registration:** 29 Oct 2026, 13:00 (must register before submitting)
- **Submission:** 12 Nov 2026, 13:00 (after institutional approval)
- **Funding starts:** 1 Oct 2027 (1-5 year grants)

---

## Section 1: Scientific Abstract

- **Limit:** Max 1 page (1 MB PDF)
- State the research question, approach, and expected significance in accessible language
- Mention all specific aims; no citations
- This is the first thing reviewers read and the basis for committee assignment

## Section 2: Research Program

- **Limit:** Max 15 pages A4 + up to 5 pages bibliography (PDF, max 16 MB)
- One PDF file containing all subsections below
- Figures and illustrations count toward the 15-page limit
- The research plan must be integrative: one coherent project, not a collection of unrelated parts

### 2a: Scientific Background

- Review the state of the field and identify the gap your project addresses
- For humanities/social sciences: frame the broader program the research belongs to
- Position preliminary data or prior work strategically

### 2b: Objectives and Significance

- State the research objectives and expected significance clearly
- Explain what success looks like and why it matters to the field
- Connect objectives to the gap identified in the background

### 2c: Detailed Research Plan

- Working hypothesis, research design, and methods
- Preliminary results (if relevant); conditions and resources needed
- Expected results, potential pitfalls, and alternative approaches
- Include required permits/approvals for the research

### 2d: Bibliography

- Up to 5 printed pages (line spacing 1.0); full citation for every reference
- Include title, all authors, and the specific publication venue for each item
- Verify every reference; a fabricated citation can disqualify the proposal

## Section 3: Bibliography with Stars

- **Limit:** Separate file
- A copy of the bibliography with starred (*) publications most relevant to the proposal topic
- Must include full publication titles and all authors' names
- Serves as a committee tool: helps identify reviewers and assess alignment

## Section 4: Time Schedule

- Detail the stages of the research in a table; include verbal explanatory notes
- Must cover the full grant period (1-5 years)
- For descriptive/non-linear research, note that timelines are approximate but still include one

## Section 5: Budget

- **Currency:** NIS
- **Personnel:** Scholarships or salary for research team; PI cannot draw salary from the grant
- **Tools and materials:** Research consumables, professional literature, journal subscriptions (up to 15,000 NIS/year)
- **Services:** Lab services, consulting, payments for data processing
- **Other expenses:** Up to 6,000 NIS/year for student travel; no PI travel/conference costs
- **Computers:** Max 1 computer + 1 laptop/tablet per PI; for office/lab use, not home
- **Equipment:** Up to 120,000 NIS for dedicated research equipment (not counted toward annual budget cap)
- **Overhead:** 17% automatically added on top of total allocation

## Section 6: CV, Recent Grants, and Investigators

- **CV:** Max 1 page per PI; list Ph.D. and postdoctoral mentor
- **Recent ISF Grants:** Max 2 pages; report on any ISF-funded project in the last ~10 years; include outcomes and publications
- If resubmitting: explain changes from previous submission (up to 5 pages); do not mention the previous application number

## Section 7: Appendix and Collaborators

- **Collaborator letters:** Max 2 pages each
- List names of all collaborating researchers, shared projects, and co-publications from the last 5 years
- Each letter must describe the collaboration's scope and the collaborator's institution

## Section 8: Declarations

- Parallel submission: disclose overlapping active grants or pending proposals
- Industrial/commercial funding: declare if research is similar to commercially funded work
- Family ties: declare close family ties with ISF staff or governance members
- Basic research declaration: confirm this is fundamental research
- LLM use: if you used a large language model in writing, disclose this; you remain solely responsible for all content

## Section 9: Authorities' Certificates

- Research Authority must approve the proposal before submission
- Ethics, animal, and human subject approvals needed at the proposal stage
- Budget released only after all required certificates are provided to the ISF

---

## Formatting Rules

- All attachments: PDF only
- Research Program: A4, minimum 2 cm margins, minimum 11pt font, 1.5 line spacing recommended
- Figures must be high-resolution and clearly readable
- Entire proposal in English
- Maximum 4 primary investigators per proposal
- One PI can only appear as primary on one ISF proposal at a time

---

## Evaluation Criterion

The sole criterion for evaluation is scientific excellence.
